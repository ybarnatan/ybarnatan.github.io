about me

My name is Yair Barnatan and I am a M.Sc. in biology, currently pursuing a PhD degree at IFIBYNE-CONCIET-UBA, Buenos Aires, Argentina. My field of work is neuroethology and my research focuses on trying to unveil the underlying mechanism of optic-flow vision in a malacostracan model, the crab Neohelice granulata.

In addition, I am part of another project, funded by the European Commission's Marie Sklodowska-Curie Research and Innovation Staff Exchanges programme. Such focuses on studying the synaptic organization of looming detector neurons in crabs, using electron microscopy techniques.




Besides my life sciences background, I am very enthusiastic about programming and software development I am a self-learner encompassing diverse areas such as data visualization and analysis, storytelling driven by data and full-stack web development. I am also a passionate cyclist -will commute by bike at all cost!- and amateur musician.
