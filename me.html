about me

My name is Yair Barnatan and I am a biologist currently pursuing a PhD degree at IFIBYNE-CONCIET-UBA, Buenos Aires, Argentina. My field of work is neuroethology and my research focus on trying to unveil the underlying mechanism of optic-flow vision in crustaceans. Also, I am part of another project, funded by the European Commission's Marie Sklodowska-Curie Research and Innovation Staff Exchanges programme. That project focuses on studying the synaptic organization of looming detector neurons in crabs, using serial block-face scanning electron microscopy.


Besides my life sciences background, I am an enthusiastic of programming and software development, encompassing diverse areas such as data visualization and analysis, storytelling driven by data and full-stack web development.
