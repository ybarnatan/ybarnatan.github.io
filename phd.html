PhD Research

summary


Dipteran lobula plate has been time after time linked with optic flow processing and the control of optomotor responses. Crabs have a neuropil similarly located and interconnected in the optic lobes, therefore referred to also as a lobula plate.

In my on-going research, I focus on studying the role of the crustacean lobula plate on optic flow processing, using behavioral experiments as well as microscopy, histological and electrophysiological techniques. I am also deeply commited to develop code in Python and R to gather, process, perform statistics and create charts and graphs upon the generated data. Apart from the study of the lobula plate, I also am part of a collaboration with the University of Newcastle where I study the ultrastructure of crab's looming detection neruons using electron microscopy techniques.




publications

• Barnatan, Y., Tomsic, D., & Sztarker, J. (2019) Unidirectional optomotor responses and eye dominance in two species of crabs. Frontiers in Physiology.
